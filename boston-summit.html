Flourishing Summit

April 29, 2026 | Boston, MA

Hosted in partnership with MASS (Model for Architecture Serving Society)

It is our distinct pleasure to welcome you to the next Flourishing Summit. This gathering is the result of years of conversation (across disciplines, communities, and forms of life) and marks a threshold moment in what these summits are becoming.

This is an invitation into a long and continually unfolding conversation about flourishing. Our conception is ecological and anthropological: flourishing is the actualization of all beings toward their good. It is not a fixed state, but an ongoing process—one of negotiation and dialectic—between individual understanding and collective well-being, always in response to the question:

How do we live well with and for each other?

We convene in the ethos of process over state, question over declaration, and ongoingness over arrival. In partnership with kindred communities, we are cultivating space for more capacious, generous, and generative dialogue.

This summit brings together scientists, artists, philosophers, contemplatives, and cultural leaders for an intimate, plural, and searching encounter, one oriented toward understanding flourishing by experiencing it.

Framing the Day

The day is loosely organized into two movements. The morning is devoted to conceptualizing flourishing: developing the philosophical and theoretical grounding necessary to study and engage it seriously. The afternoon turns toward actualization: how these ideas take form in collective life, institutions, and emerging practices of what we might call flourishing studies.

Between these, we will cultivate different modes of attention through dialogue, questioning, and shared activity, recognizing that flourishing is something to be encountered and experienced.

Underlying this gathering are tensions we do not seek to resolve prematurely, but to inhabit:

  • efficiency | participation
  • ideal | real
  • beginnings and endings | ongoingness
  • disagreement | relationality
  • memory | relinquishing

To feel these, to live these, is part of the work. From there, new questions, new possibilities, new imaginaries can emerge.

Questions We Are Carrying

The Flourishing Summits are not oriented around answers alone, but around cultivating more generous and generative questions,those that, in asking and living them, shape who we become.

  • Is justice the growing edge of flourishing?
  • Do you feel flourishing?
  • Is emergence the signal of flourishing?
  • Over what time horizons does flourishing unfold?
  • How do we measure “life-givingness” in social and engineered systems?
  • What is the relationship between care, maintenance, and flourishing?
  • What does it mean to build a field of flourishing, and for whom?
  • What forms of cross-disciplinary engagement are required?

An Invitation

There is something counter-cultural in speaking of flourishing today—and that itself is an indictment. Yet when we open to it, even briefly, it reveals itself as deeply natural.

This gathering is an experiment in creating encounters with flourishing—moments that can be carried forward into our work, our communities, and our institutions.

Systems change, in this view, is inseparable from personal transformation: the capacity to reimagine and reshape the world begins with shifts in how we attend, relate, and imagine together.

As Danielle Allen reminds us, “There is no end to history, no state of rest for democracy.” So too with flourishing: it is not a destination, but an ongoing practice.

Anything worthwhile is worth holding in many hands. We invite you into this conversation.
